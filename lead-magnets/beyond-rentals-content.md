## The Problem with Traditional Options

### Option 1: Refinance Your Property

**Pros:**
- Access to capital
- Lower interest rates (sometimes)

**Cons:**
- Replaces your existing mortgage (lose good terms)
- Lengthy approval process (90-120 days)
- Personal financial exposure
- May trigger due-on-sale clauses
- Expensive closing costs

### Option 2: Sell the Property

**Pros:**
- Maximum liquidity
- One-time event

**Cons:**
- Lose the asset forever
- Capital gains tax (15-20% federal + state)
- Depreciation recapture tax
- No future appreciation
- May need to lease back (new landlord risk)

### Option 3: Traditional Business Loan

**Pros:**
- Don't touch property
- Standard process

**Cons:**
- High interest rates (12-25% for unsecured)
- Revenue-based (not asset-based)
- Short terms (6-18 months typical)
- UCC-1 blanket liens
- Personal guarantees always required

### The Alternative: Real Estate-Backed Business Loans

**Pros:**
- Keep your existing mortgage
- Keep your property
- Lower rates than unsecured debt (8-12%)
- Longer terms (1-5 years)
- Faster funding (30 days)
- Tax-deductible interest

**Cons:**
- Property must have equity
- Lien placed on property
- Not as cheap as refinancing (but much faster)
- Smaller universe of lenders

## Tax Considerations

**Consult your CPA, but generally:**

### Interest Deductibility
- Business loan interest is tax-deductible (unlike personal mortgage interest in many cases)
- Reduces effective cost by your marginal tax rate
- Example: 10% loan, 25% tax bracket = 7.5% effective cost

### Depreciation
- Equipment purchased can be depreciated (Section 179, bonus depreciation)
- May offset interest expense
- Can create tax loss in year 1 (good thing)

### Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)
- Generally not an issue for business loans (consult CPA)

### Property Taxes
- Lien doesn't change property tax assessment
- TI improvements may increase assessed value (varies by state)

## Risk Considerations

### What Could Go Wrong?

**Scenario 1: Business Revenue Declines**
- You have debt service obligations
- Property is at risk if you can't pay
- Mitigation: Only borrow if current cash flow supports debt service + buffer

**Scenario 2: Property Value Drops**
- Less relevant (loan is based on current value)
- Only matters if you wanted to refinance later
- Mitigation: Don't over-leverage (stay <60% LTV)

**Scenario 3: Use of Funds Doesn't Work Out**
- Equipment doesn't perform
- Acquisition doesn't integrate
- Expansion location fails
- Mitigation: Underwrite conservatively, have Plan B

**Scenario 4: Interest Rates Rise**
- Fixed-rate loan = protected
- Variable-rate loan = could increase
- Mitigation: Choose fixed if you want certainty

### How We Mitigate Risk

1. **Conservative LTV (60% max):** Substantial equity cushion
2. **DSCR Requirements (1.25+ min):** Current cash flow must support debt service + 25% buffer
3. **First Lien Position (when possible):** Priority claim on property
4. **Use of Funds Review:** Only fund growth, not survival
5. **Skin in the Game:** Borrower equity contribution for acquisitions
